FAQ

What is StarburstAI?
StarburstAI is a web dashboard for Therminos—an on-chain temperature checker for crypto prices and volatility. It shows heat bands (cold, mild, warm, hot, critical) for each registered thermometer and an aggregate risk score.
What is Therminos?
Therminos is a smart contract that stores price snapshots and computes volatility over a configurable window. From that it assigns each asset a band: cold (low volatility) through critical (very high volatility). CocoaCEV and Kika are CLI tools that talk to the same contract.
What is the risk score?
A 0–100 aggregate: we weight each band (cold=0, mild=25, warm=50, hot=75, critical=100) and average across all thermometers. Higher score means more assets are in hot or critical territory.
How do I connect?
Click Connect to link your Web3 wallet (e.g. MetaMask). Paste your deployed Therminos contract address in the header. The dashboard will read heat summary, band stats, and config from the contract.
What if I don't have a contract?
The page can run in demo mode with sample thermometers and bands so you can see the layout and risk gauge. Deploy Therminos and use CocoaCEV to register symbols and report prices for live data.
Is this financial advice?
No. StarburstAI only displays on-chain data from Therminos. It is not investment or trading advice. Always do your own research.

About StarburstAI

StarburstAI is a read-only web UI for the Therminos on-chain temperature checker. Therminos tracks price and volatility for registered symbols and assigns each a heat band. This dashboard shows the heat summary, band distribution, aggregate risk score, and contract config. It is designed to work with the CocoaCEV and Kika CLI apps, which can report prices and export data from the same contract.

Built for Therminos and CocoaCEV. Not financial advice.

How to use

1.Click Connect and approve your Web3 wallet (e.g. MetaMask).
2.Paste your deployed Therminos contract address in the input field.
3.The dashboard loads heat summary, band counts, and risk score. Click Refresh to update.
4.Use the Config tab to see contract parameters (owner, treasury, thresholds, etc.).
5.To feed data: use CocoaCEV or Kika to register thermometers and report prices as the updater.

Bands glossary

Cold
Low volatility; price moves are small relative to the threshold. Band 0.
Mild
Moderate volatility; above cold threshold but still calm. Band 1.
Warm
Elevated volatility; noticeable price swings. Band 2.
Hot
High volatility; large moves. Band 3.
Critical
Very high volatility; extreme moves. Band 4.

Thresholds (in basis points) are set on the contract (cold BPS, mild BPS, warm BPS, hot BPS). Volatility is computed from price changes over the thermometer's window and mapped to these bands.

Data explained

Volatility (bps) is derived from price changes in the thermometer's window: each change in basis points is averaged (or similar) and the result is compared to the contract's band thresholds.

Price (E8) is the latest reported price in 1e8 units (e.g. 4500000000000 = 45,000). Thermometers store history; the dashboard shows current price and band.

Risk score is (sum of band weights) / (number of thermometers), where cold=0, mild=25, warm=50, hot=75, critical=100. It gives a single number for "how hot" the whole set is.

CocoaCEV & Kika

StarburstAI reads from the same Therminos contract that the CocoaCEV and Kika CLI tools use. CocoaCEV is an upgraded CLI with risk scoring, snapshots, volatility rank, health checks, report-from-file, and chain presets. Kika is the original CLI for heat summary, reporting prices, and exporting data. Use CocoaCEV or Kika to:

  • Register thermometers (symbol hash + window blocks)
  • Report prices as the updater (single or batch)
  • Export heat summary to JSON or CSV
  • Save and load snapshots for comparison
  • Run offline simulation of bands from price series

Configure contract_address and rpc_url (or use chain presets like mainnet, sepolia) in CocoaCEV/Kika config, then run commands such as summary, risk-score, report, export.

FAQ (continued)

Why do I see symbol hashes instead of names?
Therminos stores symbols as bytes32 hashes (e.g. keccak256("BTC")). The dashboard shows a short hex prefix. CocoaCEV and Kika support a symbol_map in config to map hashes to labels like "BTC" or "ETH"; a future version of StarburstAI could allow the same.
What are the default threshold BPS values?
Typical defaults on Therminos are cold 500, mild 1500, warm 3500, hot 7000 (all in basis points). The contract owner can change them via setThresholds. The Config tab shows current values.
Can I report prices from the website?
StarburstAI is read-only. To report prices you need to be the contract's updater and use CocoaCEV or Kika with a private key (report or report-from-file commands).
What chains are supported?
Any EVM chain where Therminos is deployed. Connect your wallet on that network and paste the contract address. CocoaCEV supports chain presets (mainnet, sepolia, polygon, arbitrum, base, optimism) for the RPC URL.

Data source & flow

StarburstAI reads only from the Therminos contract. No data is sent to external servers. The flow is:

  1. You connect your Web3 wallet (e.g. MetaMask) so the app can use the provider to call the contract.
  2. You paste the Therminos contract address. The app stores it in localStorage so it can be restored on next visit.
  3. When you click Refresh (or after connecting), the app calls getHeatSummary(), getBandStats(), and getConfigSnapshot() on the contract.
  4. From getHeatSummary() we get symbol hashes, bands, volatilities (E8), and prices (E8). We render the heat table and compute the aggregate risk score.
  5. From getBandStats() we get counts per band (cold, mild, warm, hot, critical) and display them in the stat cards.
  6. From getConfigSnapshot() we show owner, treasury, guardian, updater, deploy block, threshold BPS values, report fee, max history length, and paused flag.

If no contract address is set or the contract is not deployed, the app runs in demo mode with sample thermometers and bands so you can see the layout.

Security & privacy

Does StarburstAI store my private key or seed?
No. The wallet connection uses the standard Web3 API (e.g. MetaMask). Your key never leaves your wallet. StarburstAI only requests read access to the contract; it does not send transactions unless you use a different tool that does.
What is stored in the browser?
Only the Therminos contract address in localStorage (key: starburstai_therminos_contract) so we can pre-fill the input on next visit. Optionally you can store a symbol_map in localStorage (starburstai_symbol_map) for hash-to-label mapping; the app will use it if present.
Is the contract call data sent anywhere?
Contract reads go through your wallet's provider (e.g. MetaMask's RPC). Only that RPC and the contract see the read. StarburstAI does not send the data to any third-party analytics or backend.

FAQ — Technical

What is E8 format?
Price and volatility are stored in fixed-point with 8 decimals. E8 = 10^8. So 4500000000000 means 45,000.0 (45000 * 1e8). Volatility in E8 when multiplied by 10000 (BPS) and divided by 1e8 gives basis points.
What is bytes32 symbol hash?
Therminos identifies symbols by keccak256(symbol). So "BTC" becomes a 32-byte hash. The contract stores and returns these hashes; the UI shows a short hex or a label if you've configured symbol_map in CocoaCEV/Kika and synced it (future feature).
How often does the dashboard refresh?
When connected to a contract, StarburstAI auto-refreshes every 25 seconds. You can also click Refresh at any time. In demo mode there is no auto-refresh.
Which contract ABI is used?
The script uses a minimal ABI with getRegisteredSymbols, getHeatSummary, getBandStats, getThresholds, getConfigSnapshot, and platformPaused. This matches the Therminos contract interface. If the contract is upgraded with new functions, the dashboard can be updated to call them.

Troubleshooting

"Failed to load contract" — what to check
Ensure (1) your wallet is on the same network as the contract, (2) the address is the correct Therminos deployment (not a different contract), (3) your RPC is responsive. Try refreshing the page and reconnecting.
Heat table shows "No thermometers"
The contract has no registered thermometers yet. Use CocoaCEV or Kika (as owner) to call registerThermometer(symbolHash, windowBlocks) for each asset you want to track. Then use the updater to report prices.
Risk score is 0 or all bands are cold
Either there are no price reports yet, or the reported prices are very stable so volatility is below the cold threshold. Report more data points over time so volatility can be computed.
Contract address is lost after closing the tab
We save the contract address to localStorage when you change it or click Refresh. If it still doesn't persist, check that the browser allows localStorage for this site.
MetaMask or wallet not appearing
Install the wallet extension and refresh the page. Ensure the site is not blocked by the wallet. Try a different browser if the issue continues.

Appendix: Band formula

Therminos computes volatility over a sliding window of price reports. For each pair of consecutive prices in the window, the absolute percentage change in basis points is computed. The volatility measure (e.g. average of those changes, or similar) is then compared to the four threshold BPS values (cold, mild, warm, hot). If volatility ≤ coldBps then band = cold (0). If volatility ≤ mildBps then band = mild (1). If volatility ≤ warmBps then band = warm (2). If volatility ≤ hotBps then band = hot (3). Otherwise band = critical (4). The exact formula is in the Therminos contract (Solidity).

Appendix: E8 conversion

Prices and volatility are stored in E8 (1e8 = 100,000,000). To convert price E8 to a human number: divide by 1e8. Example: 4500000000000 / 1e8 = 45,000. To convert volatility E8 to basis points: (volE8 * 10000) / 1e8. Example: 150000000 (E8) → 15 bps. To convert a human price to E8: multiply by 1e8 and round. Example: 45,000.25 → 4500025000000.

Glossary of terms

Thermometer
A registered slot in Therminos for one symbol (bytes32 hash). It has a window (in blocks), price history, and current band and volatility.
Band
An integer 0–4: cold (0), mild (1), warm (2), hot (3), critical (4). Derived from volatility vs threshold BPS.
Volatility (E8)
Fixed-point measure of price movement over the thermometer's window, in 1e8 units. Convert to bps with (volE8 * 10000) / 1e8.
BPS (basis points)
1 bps = 0.01%. Used for thresholds and volatility display.
E8
Fixed-point scale 10^8. Price E8 = price * 1e8; volatility E8 is similar.
Symbol hash
bytes32 = keccak256(symbol). Therminos uses hashes to identify assets.
Updater
Contract role allowed to call reportPrice and batchReportPrices.
Guardian
Contract role that can pause the platform and halt individual thermometers.
Risk score
In StarburstAI: (sum of band weights) / (number of thermometers), where cold=0, mild=25, warm=50, hot=75, critical=100. Result 0–100.

Example workflow (owner + updater)

Typical flow from deployment to a live dashboard.

1.Deploy Therminos contract. Constructor sets treasury, guardian, updater (e.g. your address).
2.As owner: call registerThermometer(keccak256("BTC"), 100) to add a thermometer with a 100-block window.
3.Repeat for other symbols (ETH, etc.) with desired window sizes (e.g. 12–40320 blocks).
4.As updater: use CocoaCEV or Kika to report prices. Example: report-float BTC 45000.5 (sends price in E8).
5.Contract appends price to history and recomputes volatility and band for each thermometer.
6.Open StarburstAI, connect wallet, paste contract address. Dashboard shows heat summary and risk score.
7.Keep reporting prices periodically (e.g. from an oracle or script). Bands and risk score update as volatility changes.

Summary

Therminos tracks crypto price and volatility and assigns each registered symbol a heat band from cold to critical. StarburstAI displays the heat summary table, band distribution, and an aggregate risk score (0–100). Together they form a full stack for on-chain temperature checking. Not financial advice.
